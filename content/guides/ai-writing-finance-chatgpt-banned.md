BLUF: An AI writing tool for finance teams where ChatGPT is banned has to satisfy one rule above all others — no client text, deal data, or material non-public information may leave the firm. The practical answer is an on-device setup: a menu-bar assistant on macOS pointed at a local model, so the words you select are processed on your own Mac and never reach an outside server. That is what makes it usable where the public ChatGPT web app is blocked, and it is exactly how [Nugumi](/) runs when you choose the local engine.

## Why finance teams block ChatGPT in the first place

The ban is rarely about AI being unhelpful. It is about where the text goes. When an analyst pastes a draft into a public chatbot, that text travels to a third-party server, may be logged, and could fall outside the firm's data-residency and retention obligations. For regulated work — client portfolios, M&A terms, earnings language under embargo — that is an unacceptable exposure. So security teams block the public web apps and call it a day. The need to write clear, correct English does not disappear, though; it just goes unmet.

## What "where ChatGPT is banned" really means for writing help

The constraint is narrower than it looks. The policy almost always targets data leaving the organization, not the act of using a language model. That distinction is the opening: a tool that keeps both the model and your text on your own hardware sidesteps the rule it was meant to enforce. There is no outbound call to log and nothing to retain on a vendor's servers, because nothing was sent. A [private AI writing assistant that works offline](/guides/private-ai-writing-assistant-offline) is built around exactly this property.

## The on-device option that clears the policy

The setup is straightforward on a Mac. A lightweight menu-bar app talks to a local model runtime — Ollama — that downloads an open model once and serves it from your machine. Your selected text goes to that local model and the result comes back, with no internet connection in the loop. Apple Silicon handles small and mid-size models comfortably, which covers the everyday writing finance professionals actually do. If you want the model and hardware details, the [local LLM for writing on Mac](/guides/local-llm-for-writing-mac) guide walks through them.

## What you can do: understand, rewrite, reply

Three jobs cover most desk work, and all run on-device. Understand: select a dense paragraph — a counterparty's clause, a regulator's notice — and get it explained in plain words before you act. Rewrite: take the draft you already wrote and make it read clear, natural, and professional without changing the numbers or the meaning. Reply: select an incoming message and draft a response in your own voice, then edit to taste. Because the work happens inside whatever app you are in, the text never has to move to a separate window.

## Keeping client and deal data inside the firm

The whole reason to go local is to stop sensitive text from moving, so the tool should let you fix a sentence in Outlook, Bloomberg chat, a PDF term sheet, or Slack without copying it anywhere. Select, rewrite, done — the words never leave the app, let alone the machine. For teams that occasionally handle genuinely non-sensitive copy and have an approved enterprise endpoint, the same tool can route that one task to a sanctioned cloud model and switch straight back to local for the next confidential draft. You match the engine to the data, every time.

## How to roll it out without fighting IT

Because the local mode makes no outbound call, there is little for a security team to object to — and since the code is open source, they can review it before approval. There is no signup, no account, and no vendor contract for the on-device path; the model downloads once and then runs without a connection, which the [offline AI writing app with no internet](/guides/offline-ai-writing-app-no-internet) guide covers in more depth. Start small: have one analyst install it, point it at Ollama, and test rewrite, reply, and understand on real-but-shareable work to confirm both the quality and the no-data-leaves behavior.

## Getting started

Pick the local setup if your policy bans cloud AI, install the menu-bar app and Ollama, and pull one model; the [download](/download) page lists the steps for Mac. Grant the one-time permission and the shortcut works in any app with selectable text. Confirm with your own eyes that nothing leaves the machine in local mode, then let the desk use it where ChatGPT cannot go — on the confidential drafts that needed the help most.
