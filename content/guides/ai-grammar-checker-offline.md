BLUF: An AI grammar checker that works completely offline runs the language model directly on your Mac, so it can catch errors, fix awkward phrasing, and tidy your tone with no internet connection and nothing leaving your device. For non-native professionals who write all day, that means private, reliable proofreading on a plane, behind a corporate firewall, or anywhere your text is too sensitive to upload. On macOS the practical setup is a menu-bar tool paired with a local model, which is exactly how [Nugumi](/) can polish your writing when you choose the on-device engine.

## What "completely offline" means for a grammar checker

A truly offline AI grammar checker keeps both the model and your text on your own hardware. After a one-time model download, no part of your sentence travels over the network: no API call, no server log, no third party in the loop. This is different from a browser extension that quietly sends every keystroke to the cloud. If the core checking step still phones home, the tool is not really offline, no matter how it is described.

## How an offline AI grammar checker runs on your Mac

The engine behind any offline checker is a local large language model, or LLM, that you download once and store on disk. On macOS, Ollama is the usual way to run one; it manages the model files and serves them to apps on your machine. Your writing tool sends the selected text to that local model and shows the corrected version, all without a connection. The [local LLM for writing on Mac](/guides/local-llm-for-writing-mac) guide walks through model choices and hardware fit in detail.

## Why writers want grammar checking with no internet

Privacy is the main reason people choose a private grammar checker offline. When nothing you check leaves your computer, sensitive emails, contracts, medical notes, and unreleased plans stay yours, and the data-retention question disappears because nothing was ever sent. There is also reliability: a checker that does not depend on Wi-Fi keeps working in airplane mode, on trains, and inside networks with strict rules. For anyone under an NDA, that combination of confidentiality and uptime is the whole point.

## What offline AI catches beyond basic grammar

A modern local model does far more than flag a missing comma. It catches the mistakes that traditional checkers miss for non-native writers: wrong articles, off prepositions, unnatural word order, and phrasing that reads stiff or unintentionally blunt. Because it understands context, it can also explain why a sentence sounds off and rewrite it to read clear, natural, and professional. That is closer to a careful editor than a rules engine.

## Offline AI vs. traditional grammar checkers

Classic grammar checkers run on fixed rules and are fast but literal; they rarely understand tone or intent. An offline AI grammar checker reasons about meaning, so it fixes the awkwardness a rulebook cannot name. The honest trade-off is hardware: the cloud's largest models can be marginally sharper on very long or specialized text, and a small local model runs as fast as your Mac allows. For routine professional writing, the accuracy is more than enough and the privacy gain is large, a balance the [offline AI writing app with no internet](/guides/offline-ai-writing-app-no-internet) guide explores further.
