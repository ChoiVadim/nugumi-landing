BLUF: You do not need a Grammarly Premium subscription to write clearly on a Mac. The best cheaper Grammarly alternative for Mac is usually a tool you partly pay for already (a ChatGPT or Claude plan, or a free local model) paired with a lightweight assistant that polishes your text in any app. Below are the affordable and free options that actually fix grammar, tone, and clarity, and how to choose between them.

## Why Grammarly Premium feels expensive

Grammarly Premium runs around $12 to $30 per month depending on the plan, billed yearly, and the team tiers climb higher per seat. For a single professional who mostly wants clean emails and messages, that adds up fast against features like plagiarism detection and a separate web dashboard that you rarely touch. The core value, catching mistakes and improving tone, is now available from cheaper and free tools, which is why an affordable writing assistant for Mac is worth a look before you renew. The goal here is to keep the parts you actually use every day and stop paying for the parts you do not.

## Free Grammarly alternatives that ship with your Mac

macOS already checks spelling and basic grammar system-wide, and you can turn it on under System Settings in the Keyboard and Text Input panels. Apple Intelligence Writing Tools can also rewrite and proofread selected text on supported hardware at no cost, right from the context menu. These are the truest free Grammarly alternative for Mac options, though they are lighter on tone coaching and explanations than a dedicated tool. If you write in only one app or want a no-install starting point, they are the sensible first stop before paying for anything.

## LanguageTool and open-source checkers

LanguageTool has a generous free tier and a Premium plan that costs noticeably less than Grammarly. It catches grammar and style issues in the browser and major apps, and it supports many languages, which helps multilingual writers. For a deeper breakdown of standalone checkers, see our roundup of [Grammarly alternatives for Mac](/guides/grammarly-alternatives-mac).

## Use the AI subscription you already pay for

If you have ChatGPT Plus or a Claude plan, you already own a powerful grammar and rewrite engine, so the marginal cost of using it for writing is effectively zero. Pasting your draft and asking it to fix errors and improve tone often beats a Premium checker because the model understands context, intent, and audience, not just rules. You can also ask it to keep your voice, shorten a message, or make it more formal, which a rule-based checker cannot do well. We compare this approach head-to-head in [ChatGPT vs Grammarly for emails](/guides/chatgpt-vs-grammarly-emails), and for most people it is the cheapest path since there is no second subscription to buy.

## A menu-bar assistant that works in every app

The friction with chatbots is copying text back and forth. A menu-bar tool like [Nugumi](/) removes that: you select text in any Mac app, and it can explain what a confusing message means, rewrite your own draft to sound clear and professional, or draft a reply in your style. It works in Slack, Gmail, Notion, Safari, KakaoTalk, and PDFs, and it is free during early beta with no account, which makes it one of the most affordable writing assistant for Mac setups right now.

## Run it locally for total privacy and zero cost

If cost and privacy both matter, a local model is the strongest grammarly premium alternative. With local Ollama, nothing you select leaves your computer, and there is no monthly fee at all. Nugumi supports this mode, so you can rewrite drafts and polish messages entirely on-device; you can grab it from the [download](/download) page and point it at a local model.

## How to pick for your situation as a non-native writer

Choose by where you write and how much you want to spend. If you only need quick fixes in the browser, LanguageTool's free tier is enough. If you want context-aware rewrites and explanations across every app, a single AI subscription plus a menu-bar assistant is the best budget grammar checker mac choice.
