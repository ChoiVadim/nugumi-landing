BLUF: As an ESL job seeker, the most reliable way to use an AI cover letter generator is to write your own honest draft first, then let AI rewrite it so the English reads clear, natural, and right in tone. Do not ask a tool to invent a letter from nothing — that produces generic text recruiters recognize instantly. Instead, get your real experience down, fix the phrasing that signals non-native writing, and tailor each letter to the job. This guide shows the workflow step by step.

## What an AI cover letter generator should do for ESL job seekers

A good tool does more than catch typos. As a non-native English writer, your harder problems are word choice, tone, and whether a sentence sounds natural to a hiring manager. So an AI cover letter generator for ESL job seekers should polish your draft into fluent English, flag phrasing that reads as stiff or translated, and keep your specific achievements front and center. If it only fixes grammar, it solves the easy half and leaves the part that actually loses you interviews.

## Start with your real draft, then improve it

The biggest mistake is asking AI to write the whole letter from a blank prompt. The result is fluent but hollow — full of phrases like "I am passionate about leveraging synergies" that say nothing. Instead, write three short paragraphs yourself: why you want the role, one concrete result you delivered, and why you fit this company. Then hand that draft to AI to rewrite. You keep the substance and your voice; the tool only cleans up the language.

## Fix tone and naturalness, not just grammar

Grammar errors are easy to spot. Tone is where ESL cover letters quietly go wrong: a letter reads as either too formal and robotic or too casual for the role. The right tool adjusts register so you sound confident without sounding arrogant, and warm without sounding informal. This is the same skill that matters across all work writing, covered in depth in this guide on choosing an [AI writing assistant for non-native English speakers](/guides/ai-writing-assistant-non-native-english-speakers). For a cover letter, getting tone right is often what separates a callback from silence.

## Tailor each cover letter to the job

A generic letter sent to ten companies underperforms one letter shaped to a single posting. Pull two or three exact phrases from the job description — the skills, the team, the product — and make sure your draft echoes them honestly. Then use AI to weave those points in smoothly rather than bolting them on. Technical applicants can see how role-specific phrasing works in practice in this guide for an [AI writing assistant for software developers](/guides/ai-writing-assistant-for-software-developers).

## Keep your writing private while you draft

Your cover letter can name your current employer, target salary, or reasons for leaving a job. Some AI tools send everything you write to a cloud server, which is not ideal for sensitive details. The strongest tools let you choose how text is processed: a ChatGPT or Claude subscription you already pay for, your own API key, or a fully local model so nothing you write leaves your computer. Match the engine to the document — local for anything you would not want logged.

## Final checks before you send

Before you submit, read the letter aloud once — awkward spots are easier to hear than to see. Confirm the company name and role are correct everywhere, since a leftover name from another application is an instant rejection. Check that every claim is true and specific, trim any sentence that does not earn its place, and make sure the opening line is about them, not just about you. A polished, honest, tailored letter does more for an ESL job seeker than any clever phrase a generator could invent.
