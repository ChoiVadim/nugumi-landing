BLUF: An AI writing assistant for customer support agents speeds up replies by drafting a working answer from the customer's message, explaining confusing or angry tickets in plain words, and polishing your tone before you send. The bottleneck in support is rarely knowing the answer — it is phrasing it clearly, calmly, and on-brand, ticket after ticket. Use the assistant to remove the blank-box delay and the rereading, then keep a human edit on every reply. Done right, you cut handle time without sounding like a robot.

## Start by understanding what the customer actually wants

Half of slow replies come from misreading the ticket, not from typing. Long, emotional, or poorly worded messages bury the real ask under context. Select the customer's message and have the assistant explain it in plain words: what they want, what went wrong, and what they are really asking for. This works especially well on rambling complaints or messages written by non-native customers, where the intent is easy to miss on a fast skim. Getting the ask right on the first read also prevents the back-and-forth that turns one ticket into three, which is where handle time really leaks.

## Draft the reply, then make it your own

The biggest time saver is not writing from scratch. Point the assistant at the selected message and let it reply in your style, then edit the facts — order numbers, dates, policy details — and trim anything generic. Starting from a working draft beats a blank box on every ticket, and it is the same workflow that helps an [AI email assistant for founders](/guides/ai-email-assistant-for-founders) clear an inbox fast. The rule never changes: the assistant drafts, you verify and own the final words.

## Keep the tone calm, especially on angry tickets

When a customer is furious, the hard part is not the answer — it is not matching their heat. Have the assistant draft a calm, professional version, then check that it acknowledges the problem before it explains the fix. Read the complaint clearly first so you respond to the real issue, not the tone. A short line of genuine ownership early in the reply does more to settle a customer than a paragraph of policy. A measured reply de-escalates; a defensive one adds a second ticket.

## Polish wording without losing your brand voice

Raw AI output can read flat or generic, which is the fastest way to sound like a script. Use the assistant to rewrite and polish your own draft so it reads clear and natural, then cut filler and add the one human line that fits your team's voice. For non-native agents, this also fixes the small grammar and tone slips that make replies feel off — the same gains covered in this guide for [non-native English speakers](/guides/ai-writing-assistant-non-native-english-speakers). Polish the phrasing, keep the personality.

## Work right inside the tools you already use

You should not have to copy text into a separate window and back. A tool like [Nugumi](/) works on any selectable text on your Mac — Zendesk, Intercom, Gmail, Front, Slack, even a PDF a customer attached — so you select the message, trigger the action, and paste the result without leaving the ticket. It also adapts the tone to the app you are in, which keeps a Slack reply casual and a customer email professional.

## Reuse it for macros, not just one-offs

The same drafting and polish habit makes your canned responses better over time. When a macro reads stiff or dated, select it and have the assistant rewrite it to sound clearer and warmer, then save the improved version back. Over a quarter this quietly lifts the quality of every templated reply your team sends, which matters more than shaving seconds off a single ticket.
