BLUF: If you want a Grammarly alternative that works in every app on Mac — not just a browser tab or a single editor — look for a system-wide tool that acts on text you select anywhere, fixes tone as well as grammar, and lets you choose how your text is processed. Grammarly's coverage on macOS is uneven once you leave the browser, so the real test is whether you get help in the window you are already typing in. This guide explains what to look for and how to decide without overthinking it.

## Why coverage is the requirement that matters most

You write in dozens of places on a Mac, and most of them are not the browser: native Slack, KakaoTalk, Telegram, Discord, VS Code, Notes, and PDFs. A grammarly alternative that works in every app on mac removes the copy-paste-back loop that breaks your focus dozens of times a day. The real test of any writing tool is not how good the suggestions look in one window, but whether you actually get help in the window you are already typing in.

## What Grammarly does well, and where it stops on Mac

Grammarly is genuinely good at spelling, grammar, and some clarity issues, and its browser extension covers Gmail, web apps, and Google Docs reliably. The gap shows up the moment you leave the browser: many native macOS apps get partial or no coverage, and you end up pasting text into the Grammarly editor and back. For people who live in chat apps and code editors, that is most of the working day, which is exactly why a writing assistant that works in any Mac app feels like a different category of tool.

## What to look for in a Grammarly alternative that works everywhere

Prioritize a tool that operates system-wide on selected text rather than one tied to a browser extension or a single editor. A system-wide grammar and writing tool for Mac should let you select any sentence, trigger an action, and paste the result back without switching apps. Bonus points if it adapts to the app you are in — casual in Slack, professional in a client email — instead of forcing one style everywhere. For a wider view of the options, see [Grammarly alternatives for Mac](/guides/grammarly-alternatives-mac).

## Tone and clarity, not just red underlines

Grammar checkers catch mistakes well but handle tone poorly, and tone is where writing most often goes wrong — especially for non-native speakers. A strong alternative should rewrite a blunt message so it reads natural and professional, explain text you do not fully understand, and draft a reply in your own voice. If a tool only underlines errors, it is solving the easy half of the problem and leaving the part that actually changes how your message lands.

## Keep your text private: cloud or local

A grammarly alternative without a browser extension can also be more private, depending on the engine behind it. The strongest tools let you choose: a ChatGPT or Claude subscription you already pay for, your own API key, or a fully local model so nothing you select leaves your computer. Match the choice to the document — cloud for everyday messages, local for contracts, client names, or internal plans.

Before you commit to any Grammarly replacement, confirm it works in every app rather than one editor, fixes tone and clarity instead of only spelling, adapts to the app you are in, and lets you choose cloud or local processing.
