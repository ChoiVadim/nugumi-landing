BLUF: To reply to your boss politely on KakaoTalk, use polite Korean speech (존댓말), keep their honorific title like 팀장님 or 부장님 instead of a first name, acknowledge the message clearly, and answer the actual ask in one or two short lines. KakaoTalk is the default work messenger in most Korean offices, so the etiquette that governs spoken Korean applies in the chat too — a bare "네" or an English "ok" can land as curt. Aim for warm, respectful, and easy to act on.

## Why politeness on KakaoTalk works differently in Korea

In Korean workplaces, KakaoTalk is not casual chat — it is where managers assign tasks, ask for updates, and expect timely answers. Politeness is built into the grammar itself, so a reply that feels perfectly neutral in English can read as rude if you drop the polite endings. For expats, the safest default is to treat every message to your boss the way you would treat a formal email, even when it is a single line. Understanding that gap is the first step toward KakaoTalk work etiquette that does not accidentally offend.

## Match the formality level your boss expects (존댓말)

Korean has two broad registers: 존댓말 (polite speech) and 반말 (casual speech). With a boss you always use 존댓말 — verbs ending in -요 or the more formal -습니다/-ㅂ니다. Even short confirmations get softened: instead of a blunt "ㅇㅇ" (yeah), write "네, 알겠습니다" (yes, understood). When in doubt, choose the more formal ending — over-politeness is forgiven, under-politeness is remembered.

## Use honorifics and titles, not first names

Koreans rarely address a manager by first name. Use their job title plus the honorific suffix -님: 팀장님 (team lead), 부장님 (department head), 대리님, or 선배님 for a senior colleague. Open your reply with the title — "팀장님, 확인했습니다" — much as you might start an English message with "Hi [Name]." Getting the title right signals that you understand the hierarchy, which on KakaoTalk often matters more than the exact wording that follows.

## Structure a polite reply your boss can act on

A good reply does three things: acknowledge, answer, and close. Acknowledge with 네 or 확인했습니다, answer the question or task directly, and close with a courteous line such as 감사합니다 or 수고하셨습니다 ("thank you for your hard work"). Keep it short — KakaoTalk is for quick exchanges — but never so short that it reads as cold. If you need more time, say so politely rather than leaving the message on read.

## A polite KakaoTalk reply example

Suppose your team lead writes "내일까지 보고서 부탁해요" (please send the report by tomorrow). A polite reply: "팀장님, 네 확인했습니다. 내일 오전까지 보고서 정리해서 보내드리겠습니다. 감사합니다!" That covers the title, a clear acknowledgment, a specific commitment with a time, and a warm close — under twenty words and unmistakably respectful. If you have to push back, soften it: "팀장님, 혹시 모레까지 가능할까요? 오늘 다른 마감이 겹쳐서요." This is the same instinct that helps you [politely decline a request](/guides/politely-decline-request-slack) anywhere you work.

## Mind timing, read receipts, and after-hours messages

KakaoTalk shows a "1" next to your boss's message until you open it, so leaving it unread for long can look like you are ignoring them. Reply promptly during work hours, and if a message arrives late at night, a short "네, 내일 확인하겠습니다" beats either silence or a half-finished answer. Quick replies are expected, but not at the cost of correctness — a thoughtful message a few minutes later is better than an abrupt one sent instantly. Reading the room on timing is as much a part of the etiquette as the words you choose.
