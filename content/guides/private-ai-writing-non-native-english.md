BLUF: Private AI writing help for non-native English speakers at work means a tool that fixes your grammar, tone, and clarity while keeping every word on your own Mac. When it runs a local model, nothing you select, rewrite, or reply to leaves the device, so you can polish a sensitive email or a client message without sending it to a remote server. The practical setup today is a menu-bar assistant paired with a local model through Ollama, which is exactly how [Nugumi](/) can run when you choose the on-device engine.

## Why non-native professionals need privacy, not just polish

Plenty of tools will fix your English. Far fewer let you fix it without uploading the text first. That gap matters most for non-native speakers at work, because the messages you most want help with are often the sensitive ones: a reply to a difficult client, a note about a deadline slip, a comment on an unreleased plan. A confidential AI writing tool for non-native speakers removes the trade-off between sounding professional and protecting the content, since the words never travel anywhere they should not.

## What "private" actually means here

Private means the language model and your text both stay on your hardware. After the model downloads once, there is no API call and no server log for the core writing step. This is different from a cloud tool that simply promises not to keep your data — here there is nothing to keep, because nothing was sent. If a tool still sends your draft out to generate the result, it is not truly private, however it is marketed. The [private AI writing assistant that works offline](/guides/private-ai-writing-assistant-offline) guide covers this distinction in more depth.

## The three jobs it handles on-device

Most daily writing comes down to three tasks, and all of them run locally. Understand: select a confusing sentence and get a plain-words explanation, so unfamiliar phrasing stops slowing you down. Rewrite: polish your own draft so it reads clear, natural, and professional without losing your meaning or making it sound robotic. Reply: draft a response to a message in your own style, then edit to taste. With on-device English writing help for work, each of these runs entirely on your Mac, inside whatever app you are already in.

## How it runs locally on a Mac

The common setup pairs a lightweight menu-bar app with a local model runtime called Ollama. Ollama downloads an open model — a small-to-mid size model runs well on Apple Silicon — and serves it on your machine. The writing app sends your selected text to that local model and shows the result, with no connection involved. If you want the full picture of model choices and hardware fit, the [local LLM for writing on Mac](/guides/local-llm-for-writing-mac) guide walks through it.

## Fixing the mistakes non-native writers actually make

Generic spell-check misses the errors that quietly mark writing as non-native: missing or wrong articles, off prepositions, tense drift, and a tone that lands too blunt or too formal. A secure writing assistant for ESL employees catches these in context and rewrites toward something a native colleague would send, while keeping your intent. Because it is app-aware, it can read that a Slack message wants warmth and a contract reply wants precision, then adjust accordingly.

## Works in every app you already use

The point of a menu-bar assistant is that it lives everywhere you type — Slack, Gmail, Notion, Safari, KakaoTalk, Telegram, PDFs, even VS Code. You should never have to copy a sensitive draft into a separate website to clean it up, which would defeat the privacy you went local for in the first place. Select, fix, done, with nothing leaving the window or the machine.

The smart move is to match the engine to the document: local for anything confidential, cloud when a routine message needs the strongest model and speed matters more than secrecy.
